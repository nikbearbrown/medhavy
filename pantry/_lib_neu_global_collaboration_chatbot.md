NEU Global Collaboration Chatbot — 
1) Executive Summary
Purpose: A secure, university‑wide conversational assistant that surfaces people, projects, courses, and opportunities across Northeastern’s global network, with a special focus on AI‑in‑education and experiential learning matches (faculty ⇄ students ⇄ industry).
Value: Shortens time from idea → collaboration; increases grant velocity, cross‑campus teaching innovation, and co‑op/project placements aligned to college priorities.
Differentiator: Integrates NEU’s intelligent textbooks and case‑study generators so faculty can instantly embed contemporary AI learning objects and project briefs into classes.
Governance: Opt‑in data sharing, role‑based access, auditable retrieval; alignment with FERPA/GDPR and IRB where applicable.
Pilot: 90‑day scoped rollout across 2–3 colleges + one industry vertical + one non-Boston campus; success defined by collaboration matches, course integrations, and industry projects initiated.

2) What the Chatbot Does
A. Collaboration Discovery
Conversational search over grants, publications, centers, labs, syllabi, evaluations (if permitted), and self‑declared interests.
Suggests who to talk to and what to do next (warm intros, shared interests, prior collaborations, funding cycles, relevant courses).
B. AI‑in‑Education Enablement
Recommends NEU‑built case‑study generators and intelligent textbooks tools mapped to course outcomes and program accreditation needs.
Auto‑drafts "+AI modules" for syllabi with assessment rubrics and academic integrity guidelines.
C. Experiential Learning Matching
Students: skill‑, interest‑, and location‑aware matching to faculty projects, centers, and industry briefs.
Faculty/Researchers: staffing suggestions (UG/Grad/PhD), IRB/ethics flags, and course‑embedded project templates.
Companies: secure portal to post scoped experiential projects, NDA handling, and team formation workflows.
D. Proactive Signals
Notifies users of new grants, center calls, capstone themes, and relevant collaborators; builds semester‑ready project kits.

3) Primary User Journeys
Faculty: “I teach X and research Y—who at NEU can co‑teach, co‑author, or co‑advise with me, and what AI learning objects can I drop into my course next term?”
Students: “I’m in program Z, want hands‑on work in A/B; show me projects, centers, and mentors across global campuses that fit my skills and schedule.”
Companies/Partners: “We need a proof‑of‑concept on topic Q. Help us scope a student‑faculty team and align timeframes with academic calendars.”
Deans/Admins: “Where are the cross‑college opportunities this term? Which courses and centers are best positioned for high‑impact industry collaborations?”
