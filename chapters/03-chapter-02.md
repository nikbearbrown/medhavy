## A note about AI

The chapter is placeholder. The note is too. But the note's question is still real: what work does this chapter intend to do, and where does the model help or damage the doing of it? Answer that question before drafting, and the drafting becomes easier.

Where the model genuinely helps: producing a list of the questions this chapter could plausibly cover, ranked by how distinctive each would be to Medhavy's argument. The exercise sharpens scope.

Where the model does damage: producing the chapter itself before the scope question is answered. Fluency without scope is the failure mode of every model-drafted chapter.

The rule: scope first, then draft. The model can help with both, but not in the same step.
