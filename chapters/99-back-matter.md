## A note on the absence of an index

This book is published primarily through Kindle and online distribution, and is designed for tight integration with [Medhavy](https://www.medhavy.com/) as a live, instrumented reading environment. Both of those facts make a traditional back-of-book printed index the wrong tool for the job, and the omission is deliberate rather than expedient.

A printed index serves a specific need: it lets a reader, holding a bound physical book, locate every passage where a term or concept appears. The Kindle edition replaces that need with full-text search across the entire book and the reader's personal highlights. The Medhavy edition extends that further. A reader who underlines a phrase in Medhavy is logged — with her consent — by the platform, which can then surface the prior passages where the same concept appeared, the chapter exercises that test the concept, and the prior conversations she has had with the Ask AI mode about that concept. The platform's recall is more precise than a back-of-book index could be, and it is personalized to the reader who built it.

For readers who want a static index for offline reference — for instance, instructors who are planning a course around the book — the chapter Lexicon (Appendix A) serves as the conceptual index, and the Notes section above (when populated) serves as the citation index.

The book is, in this sense, two artifacts at once. The Kindle and EPUB editions are the static text. The Medhavy edition is the live, conducted experience the book describes from the inside.

*मेधावी (Medhavy)* — from Sanskrit, meaning *intelligent* or *intellectually brilliant* — is the system that conducts the reading, the experiment, and the disclosure.

## Glossary

A short reference for the load-bearing vocabulary the book teaches. Cross-references point to the chapter where the term is fully developed. Appendix A (The Medhavy Lexicon) is the more thorough version with worked examples.

**Adaptive engine.** The component of the architecture that decides which mode plays for which learner on which concept at which moment. In Medhavy, implemented as a within-learner contextual bandit (Chapter 8). The engine's reward signal is built from GLP components, not engagement metrics.

**Ash.** A composite case used throughout the book — a first-year medical student working through pharmacokinetics. Ash is the *full-architecture-required* end of the calibration spectrum. (Chapters 5, 8, others.)

**Ask AI.** One of the four modes. The context-anchored RAG-grounded AI tutor that ships with every Medhavy textbook page. (Chapter 7.)

**Bastani 2025.** Bastani, H., Bastani, O., Sungu, A., Ge, H., Kabakcı, Ö., & Mariman, R. (2025). *PNAS* 122(26), e2422633122. The cleanest single piece of current evidence that the conductor's design decisions — specifically, the system prompt wrapping a given AI model — determine whether AI-assisted learning helps or harms. Two prompt wrappers, same model, 17-percentage-point gap. (Chapter 4 and onward.)

**Book Library.** The content layer of the four-layer architecture — the actual textbook chapters, exercises, video, and interactive elements the platform delivers. (Chapters 5, 10.)

**Case Study.** One of the four modes. Solo asynchronous AI-facilitated case-based learning on faculty-curated cases. (Chapter 7.)

**Conductor frame.** The book's central concept. Medhavy is the conductor; the intelligent textbook is whatever the conductor conducts. The conductor decides which instrument plays based on evidence, not assumption, and adjusts as the evidence changes. (Chapter 1.)

**Cost-collapse asymmetry.** The pattern by which AI-driven production cost reductions lower the burden of proof for some categories of educational content (low-risk, reversible, supplementary) while leaving it unchanged for others (high-risk, mortality-adjacent, foundational). The asymmetry is the design rule for the AI-generation policy. (Chapter 10.)

**Decoupling problem.** The condition, post-LLM, in which the artifact a student produces is no longer reliable evidence of the cognitive process that produced it. A well-structured essay can be generated in seconds; the essay is no longer evidence the student wrote it. The decoupling is what makes the GLP measurement layer necessary. (Chapter 6.)

**Engagement-as-proxy-for-learning.** The field's dominant measurement failure. Engagement metrics — time on platform, clicks, completion rates — sometimes correlate with learning and sometimes do not, and platforms built to optimize for them produce systems that maximize the trace without producing the thing the trace was supposed to indicate. (Chapter 2 and onward.)

**Experiment-as-product.** The position the platform takes about its own pedagogical commitments. Medhavy does not pick a model and defend it; it runs a continuously controlled experiment on each learner, each teaching approach, and the AI itself as it evolves. (Chapter 1.)

**Fluency trap.** The phenomenon by which smooth AI-generated explanations produce genuine *feelings* of understanding in the learner without producing the underlying cognitive integration that durable understanding requires. The trap is real, it is well-documented in cognitive science, and the GLP measurement layer is partly designed to detect when a learner has fallen into it. (Chapter 6.)

**Four layers.** The architecture of an intelligent textbook in the Medhavy specification — Book Library, Tic TOC (Curriculum Design), Adaptive Engine, Measurement Layer. The layers are a loop, not a stack; a break anywhere degrades the whole. (Chapter 5.)

**Four modes.** The instruments the conductor selects between for any given learner-concept pair — Ask AI, Case Study, Quiz Me, Glimmer. Each mode activates a different cognitive operation. (Chapter 7.)

**Genuine Learning Probability (GLP).** The composite measurement-layer signal Medhavy uses to estimate whether genuine learning is occurring, computed from seven friction-trace components (Y1 through Y7). Used by the adaptive engine as the reward signal in place of engagement metrics. (Chapter 6, Appendix C.)

**Glimmer.** One of the four modes. An ill-structured open-ended generative assignment, scaffolded heavily early and faded as competence builds. The mode most subject to the novice-anxiety failure if deployed without phase gates. (Chapter 7.)

**IDK-IDK pattern.** The book's diagnostic example of engagement-as-proxy-for-learning failure. Named after the Khanmigo deployment data in which students typed *I don't know, I don't know* into the Socratic prompt and were logged as engaged. (Chapters 1, 2, 6.)

**Instructor.** The second of the three audiences in the conductor frame's ordering. Domain experts who build the learning experience the platform delivers. (Chapter 1.)

**Learner.** The first of the three audiences in the conductor frame's ordering. The human sitting with the material, trying to understand something. The platform's primary obligation. (Chapter 1.)

**Organization.** The third of the three audiences in the conductor frame's ordering. Institutions that purchase the platform and impose compliance, branding, and data-governance constraints. Third because their satisfaction is necessary for deployment but is downstream of whether the deployment produces learning. (Chapter 1.)

**Phase gate.** A measurement-driven rule that prevents a mode from being available to a learner until a specific cognitive precondition has been met. Quiz Me is gated until the section has been read (Y1). Glimmer is gated until the concept has been built (Y7). Case Study is gated until representation is sufficient (Y3). (Chapter 7.)

**Priya.** A composite case used throughout the book — a nineteen-year-old job seeker in Hyderabad working from an Anki deck and a weekly Humanitarians AI volunteer call. Priya is the *full-architecture-not-required* end of the calibration spectrum. (Chapter 3 and references throughout.)

**Quiz Me.** One of the four modes. Retrieval practice scheduled via the FSRS spaced-repetition algorithm, with a load-bearing due-today counter for habit formation. (Chapter 7.)

**Three questions (in order).** The conductor frame's diagnostic. Learner first, instructor second, organization third. The order is the argument. (Chapter 1.)

**Tic TOC.** The curriculum design layer of the architecture, named for the table-of-contents-as-instrument framing. The score the conductor reads to know which concepts to conduct in which order. Also the meta-instrument the book itself was produced through. (Chapter 9.)

**Within-learner bandit.** The contextual-bandit formulation in which the adaptive engine learns a per-learner reward function over (mode × concept × time) cells, rather than generalizing across learners. The architectural commitment that distinguishes Medhavy's engine from most adaptive platforms. (Chapter 8.)
